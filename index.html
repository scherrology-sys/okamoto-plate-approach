Fastball · The Result
98.4 avg EV
When pitchers go fastball and he makes contact, the results are hard. Two home runs at 110.4 and 107.7. Several additional balls in play above 100 mph. The fastball is where he's producing the most damage when he gets to it.
Glove-Side Breaking · The Result
62% / 75%
Pitchers seem to have settled on the slider and cutter as their primary put-away pitches. He wasn't swinging at either in the debut series. He is now, and the whiff rates when he does swing, 62% on sliders and 75% on cutters, suggest that sequence is working so far.
Sinker · A Second Plan
3 GIDP
Some pitchers are going a different direction: heavy sinker down in the zone. Three grounded-into-double-plays in 60 PA. Not every staff is committing to the breaking ball sequence. The sink below the belt has been effective at generating ground contact.
The Overall Picture
21 K · 6 BB
Pitchers have two working plans against him: breaking balls away to generate misses, or sinkers down to generate ground contact. The fastball is where those plans break down. Six walks show the count management is still driving plate appearances even with the high strikeout total.
What the map shows so far: Whiffs tend to cluster away and outside the zone on breaking and off-speed pitches. Contact damage tends to cluster middle-to-inner half on fastballs. Pitchers seem to be working the edges and tunneling from the fastball to the breaking ball rather than challenging him in the zone. How that plays out over a full season is still very much an open question.
